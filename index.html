About

Originally from a small town nestled in northwestern Illinois, I am looking to transition into the tech industry. After High School, I spent some time in the U.S. Army to raise money for college. While I was at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, I planned on pursuing a career in education, and I graduated with a degree in History and a minor in French. Little did I know then, I experienced something that would change my life: a Computer Science class taught in Python.

After spending sometime in classrooms in France and in special needs classrooms, I decided that I loved learning more than I enjoy teaching. Don't get me wrong, I get great satisfaction from sharing things that I'm passionate about and helping others succeed, but I'd rather be the student in the educational setting. Software Development allows me this chance because there is always something new to learn. It gives an outlet to express myself creatively and use deep-thinking logic in a professional setting.

Obviously, I had to take action, and something came back to me after reminiscing on my college days--that computer science class. Computers have been a part of my life since around the age of seven, and I have been on the internet since middle school. It all started to piece together, so I started a Fullstack Web Development bootcamp, which taught an Express.js MVC stack and MERN stack app running GraphQL.

Since graduation, I've been working on Data Structures and Algorithms, and have recently began working with Java Spring Boot. I also have experience with Django! I'm interested in web APIs, SaaS and eCommerce, Databases digital and pixel art, retro aesthetics, interactive fiction, and cats.
